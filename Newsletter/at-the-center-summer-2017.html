In These Times

Make no mistake – the failure of the U.S. Senate to move forward on the repeal of the Affordable Care Act is a victory for organizing. For every one of the 260 days since November 9, 2016, we have been in the fight of our lives. And the work has paid off.

Let me take a moment to highlight three critical ways in which this struggle was defined by grassroots organizing. First and foremost, stories of real people raised with the support of CCC and other allies connected with Americans across the country. The human focus of this debate fundamentally spiked the popularity of Obamacare and torpedoed voter support for every twisted version of the GOP’s effort to take health insurance away from millions. CCC On The Move
 

The Center for Community Change, its political sister organization, the Center for Community Change Action and our grassroots partners worked for months to pressure U.S. Senators to vote no on a health care bill that would cut Medicaid and force millions of people to go without visits to the doctor or needed medicine or medical care. We pressured lawmakers with letters, phone calls, visits to their offices, marches and a series of other actions. Our collective efforts show the power of the people when they speak up and make their voices heard.

CCC and CCCA organizers worked with our partners in Ohio, Maine, Nevada, West Virginia, Arizona, Kentucky and many other states to hold actions that let their GOP Senators know how important Medicaid is to the constituents they serve. Members of the Ohio Organizing Collaborative came to DC on June 21 to attend Sen. Rob Portman’s constituent coffee. They let Portman know why he should vote no on the bill and staged a sit-in that was covered by Think Progress ...
