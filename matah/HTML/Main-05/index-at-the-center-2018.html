In These Times: A Message by Dorian Warren
When I joined the Community Change and Community Change Action team in November 2016, our country was in a bleak place. Two years later, as I step into the role of president of Community Change, I’m much more hopeful. This year’s elections saw unprecedented wins in unexpected places. This year, the tide began to turn.
Pundits have called it a blue wave or a blue ripple—making it sound like something that “just happened.” It didn’t. The progressive wins we saw on November 6 were the result of a massive effort to engage, motivate, and mobilize voters in our communities. This was about people of color voting in record numbers. About women, particularly women of color, running—and winning—in places written off by the conventional wisdom. These wins were about people like you who believed that the electorate can and should look more like the country.
The thing is, winning elections is not the same as winning justice. That’s why Community Change Action is focused on changing the electorate as part of a larger fight to change the rules of our democracy and our economy to reflect the interests of immigrants and young people, men and women of color, and families barely getting by.

Lifting Up Our Voices
Community Change Action experimented with new digital communication strategies. Using a new simple selfie video tool, we recruited grassroots community members to tell their own stories about why they were voting and share these messages with their friends and family, encouraging them to join in the ballot box. We also helped 14 Communications Fellows to share their lived experiences of what is at stake in more than 300 videos, graphics, and op-eds that ran in state and national news sites, such as Latino Rebels, the Orlando Sentinel and Las Vegas Sun.
